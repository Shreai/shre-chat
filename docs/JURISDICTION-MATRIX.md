# Jurisdiction Matrix

> Template only. This is not legal advice.
> Fill this per product before launch and have local counsel review the final package.

## Purpose

This matrix records which legal documents, notices, and reviews apply per country or market.

## Columns

- jurisdiction
- product or tenant
- governing law
- venue or dispute model
- consumer terms required
- privacy notice required
- employment or contractor issues
- data transfer restrictions
- regulated-data notes
- local counsel review status
- launch approval status

## Example Entries

| Jurisdiction | Product | Governing Law | Venue | Required Docs | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| United States | any | contract-specific | contract-specific | terms, privacy, IP, security addendum | review state-specific rules |
| Canada | any | contract-specific | contract-specific | terms, privacy, IP, security addendum | review federal and provincial rules |
| United Kingdom | any | contract-specific | contract-specific | terms, privacy, IP, security addendum | review UK GDPR and consumer rules |
| European Union / EEA | any | contract-specific | contract-specific | terms, privacy, DPA, IP, security addendum | review transfer and controller/processor language |
| India | any | contract-specific | contract-specific | terms, privacy, IP, security addendum | review local data and contract rules |

## Operating Rules

- never assume one country's terms are valid in another market
- add a row before launch in a new market
- if a market handles regulated data, require a separate approval step
- keep local legal review status visible in the release checklist
