# Risk Register

This document records the main risks for a project, product, or change request.
It keeps risks visible without adding a lot of process.

## Why It Exists

- identify what could go wrong
- assign an owner to each major risk
- record the mitigation and trigger
- make risk review part of delivery, not an afterthought

## Risk Fields

- risk id
- title
- category
- likelihood
- impact
- owner
- affected project or workspace
- mitigation
- trigger
- status
- review date

## Suggested Categories

- scope
- budget
- schedule
- technical
- security
- compliance
- legal
- data
- operations
- vendor
- adoption
- support

## Review Rules

- keep the register small and current
- record only material risks, not every possible annoyance
- update the register when scope, architecture, or environment changes
- link risks to the docs or controls that reduce them
- escalate high-risk items through governance review

## Practical Thresholds

Treat a risk as material if it can:

- delay launch
- increase cost materially
- break the security or compliance posture
- create data loss or service outage
- affect customer trust or adoption

## Risk Response Options

- avoid
- mitigate
- transfer
- accept

## Rule

If a risk can affect scope, cost, timeline, security, or launch readiness, it belongs in the risk register.
